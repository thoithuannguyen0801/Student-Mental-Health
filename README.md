**Student Wellbeing & Success Analysis**
Executive Summary:  An integrated data analysis investigating the interplay between study habits, mental health (Depression/Anxiety), and long-term socio-economic outcomes.

Tech Stack & Workflow
- Languages/Libs: Python (Pandas, Scikit-learn, Matplotlib, Seaborn).
- Processing: Data cleaning, Median Imputation, Label Encoding, StandardScaler.
- Modeling: Comparative analysis of Logistic Regression, Decision Tree, and KNN.

Key Findings
- Academic Engine: Study time strongly predicts exam scores (r = +0.83).
- Mental Health Gap: ~34% prevalence of distress vs. only 5.9% seeking treatment.
- Top Predictor: Marital Status and Anxiety are the strongest predictors of depression risk, while CGPA has a negligible impact.
- ROI: Postgraduate degrees correlate with significantly higher income (~$146k).

Model Performance (Accuracy)
- Logistic Regression: 72.7%
- Decision Tree: 63.6%
- KNN: 63.6%

Strategic Impact: Shifting university support from academic-only metrics to proactive, low-barrier mental health interventions to safeguard student productivity and success.
